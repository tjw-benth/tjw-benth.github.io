Tom Williams

Arctic Marine Ecologist / Aspiring Data Scientist

Biography

I'm Tom Williams, a postgraduate researcher at School of Ocean and Earth Science at the University of Southampton, UK. I am a marine biologist by training and a striving quantitative ecologist. My work revolves around assessing the responses of marine biodiversity to climate change and resulting consequences for ecosystem functioning.

Research Interests

I have broad interests in marine benthic ecology, Arctic ecosystems and data science. I am well versed in the use of experimentally manipulated environments to evaluate organism responses to disturbances, and the repercussions of these responses on ecosystem functioning. I have also developed trait-based computational models that explore the functional ramifications of cascadal biodiversity loss, by incorporating extinction orders based on vulnerabilities and species interactions.
